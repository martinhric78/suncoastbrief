🎣 Tides & Fishing

Tarpon are rolling — and the bait is everywhere

— The Tackle Box
🐟🐠🐟🐡🦐🦈🐠🦀🦀
🎯 Catch of the day

🟢 What's biting: It's tarpon time — the big poons (80–120 lb) are working the beaches, passes, and the Skyway. Redfish and trout are chewing the flats early on moving water, with mangrove snapper stacked around any structure. Bait's thick: greenbacks and threadfins everywhere, and the pass crabs are running for the tarpon.
Best plan in this heat: fish dawn, dusk, or under the dock lights at night. Work moving tides — the morning high and the evening change. Snook are closed for the summer (catch & release till Sept 1), but they're stacked in the passes if you want the fight.

Where you live · Gibsonton

How "Gibtown" became the Sideshow Capital of the World

That quiet stretch of Gibsonton along the Alafia was once the most famous carnival town in America. From the 1940s on, circus and sideshow performers wintered here — and the town wrote special zoning so neighbors could keep elephants and carnival rides in their front yards. The post office even built a low counter for residents with dwarfism, the first of its kind in the country.

Its most beloved couple were Al and Jeanie Tomaini — "The Giant" at 7'4" and "The Half-Girl" at 2'6" — who ran the Giant's Camp fishing lodge. Al gave the town its first ambulance and was its volunteer fire chief.

📍 Still here: the Tomaini marker on US-41, and the Showmen's Museum.

A little Florida wonder

Did you know our corner of Florida sits in "Lightning Alley"? The stretch between Tampa and Orlando gets more lightning per square mile than almost anywhere in the U.S. — it's why those afternoon storms are so reliable, and why the hockey team's named the Lightning. ⚡
